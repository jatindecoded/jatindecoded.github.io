5 Reasons why Separators Fail.

Published on: 19 May 2025

1. Filtration Grade Mismatch — the Silent Energy Thief

If a separator’s pore size isn’t right, either oil escapes or energy consumption rises—both costly outcomes.

Kenrax Fix

We supply replacements engineered to match your compressor’s original filtration grade, optimizing performance and efficiency from the start

2. Thermal Fatigue in Sealing Components

Compressors run hot. Standard sealants and gaskets can embrittle, bleed, or “cold‑flow,” releasing particles that clog the media and shorten element life.

Kenrax Fix

High‑temperature‑rated sealing materials hold their shape under continuous cycling, keeping the media clear and extending service intervals.

3. Oil‑Media Incompatibility

Synthetic, mineral, food‑grade, fire‑resistant—modern oils span a chemistry spectrum. Pair the wrong media with the wrong lubricant and binder degradation starts in weeks, not months.

Kenrax Fix

Each separator is specified only after checking the lubricant family used in your plant, ensuring long‑term compatibility and stable performance.

4. Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) Pinholes

Fast airflow can build tens of kilovolts on the separator core. Microscopic arcs punch pinholes in the media—undetectable until oil mist clouds the discharge line.

Kenrax Fix

Design measures dissipate charge safely to ground, preventing arc damage and preserving continuous filtration efficiency.

5. Counterfeit “Look‑Alike” Parts

They might wear a familiar label, but shortcuts in paper density, adhesive, and burst strength lurk beneath. The result: early failure, higher oil carry‑over, and potential warranty issues on your compressor.

What Consistent Separator Performance Delivers

  • Lower Energy Bill: Stable low ΔP trims compressor amp‑draw.
  • Cleaner Plant Air: Minimal oil vapor means healthier work zones and fewer downstream filter changes.
  • Predictable Maintenance: Extended service intervals let you plan downtime instead of reacting to it.

  • Oil top‑ups increasing between services?
